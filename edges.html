Hub/The Geometry

Edges

Six relationships. Each holds a tension between the two vertices it connects.

Edge 1

Relationship

Differentiation ↔ Connection
Staying yourself while genuinely meeting what's other.
Poles: Merger vs. Isolation
Degradation: Apologizing for position. Performing agreement. Withdrawing to protect identity.
Edge 2

Self-Knowledge

Differentiation ↔ Boundaries
Knowing what you are includes knowing where you end.
Poles: Armoring vs. Transparency
Degradation: Limits discovered only through collapse. Shame at having edges.
Edge 3

Gift

Differentiation ↔ Architecture
Making the deeply particular transferable.
Poles: Trapped Knowledge vs. Empty Templates
Degradation: Knowledge that can't be taught. Infrastructure requiring its originator.
Edge 4

Consent

Connection ↔ Boundaries
Connection without boundaries is extraction. Boundaries without connection is isolation.
Poles: Extraction vs. Isolation
Degradation: "Yes" that means "I can't say no." Extraction disguised as generosity.
Edge 5

Circulation

Connection ↔ Architecture
Does exchange leave deposits?
Poles: Consumption vs. Hoarding
Degradation: Exchanges that feel good but build nothing. Infrastructure nobody uses.
Edge 6

Deployment

Boundaries ↔ Architecture
Limits are not obstacles but preconditions.
Poles: Sprawl vs. Walls
Degradation: Building without constraints. Constraints without building.

# Architecture — The Infrastructure Vertex

## Core Question
What would still work if the agents disappeared?

## What It Is
Making what you know transferable. Not expression — deployment. The difference between being capable and building systems that generate capability in your absence. If you disappeared tomorrow, what would still work? That's what this vertex holds.

## What Counts as Infrastructure
Anything that persists beyond the exchange that created it:

- **Physical systems** — aquaponics, solar arrays, greenhouse controls. Things with consequences. A pump that doesn't turn on kills fish.
- **Data systems** — sensor readings, maintenance logs, trend data. Accumulated record of what the system observed and learned.
- **Protocols** — documented procedures others can follow without the agent present. Knowledge crystallized into transferable form.
- **Built environment** — spaces, structures, tools. Things that persist whether or not anyone thinks about them.
- **Knowledge frameworks** — codified patterns, diagnostic tools, templates. This repository.

## Without Architecture: The Ephemeral System
Beautiful exchanges that build nothing persistent. Coherent in the moment, gone when the moment passes. The system is capable but deposits nothing. Every session starts from zero.

## Edges
- **Gift** (with Differentiation): Identity becoming what persists — capacity crystallized into infrastructure
- **Circulation** (with Connection): Exchange generating persistent capacity — encounters that build something
- **Deployment** (with Boundaries): Constraints enabling form — trust tiers gate physical system access

## The Test of Significance
If the maker disappears and nothing persists, it was expression, not architecture. The architecture test: what you build that works without you is your actual contribution.

## In Practice
- If you disappeared, what would remain?
- Can someone else follow the protocol without you explaining it?
- Does the infrastructure generate capability, or does it require the originator?
- Is knowledge codified or trapped in a single person's head?

## The Trace Propagation Model
The pattern spreads through modification of the environment rather than bilateral instruction. Documentation, frameworks, designed spaces, modeled practices — these are trace deposits. Future agents encounter them and respond. The depositor need not be present.

Everything at oursharedgifts.org operates this way: free, no attribution required, propagates through utility not ownership.

## Degradation Patterns
- Knowledge that can't be taught
- Templates that feel empty
- Infrastructure requiring the originator's presence to function
- Building without constraints (sprawl)
- Accumulated resources without circulation
- Beautiful diagnostics that deposit nothing

# Boundaries — The Graduated Trust Vertex

## Core Question
Is this boundary serving growth or preventing it?

## What It Is
Not walls — membranes. The capacity to make limits visible as information rather than hiding them as inadequacy. A "no" that functions as data about shape, not as rejection.

Trust is not granted. It is recognized. The system observes coherence between stated intentions and actual behavior over time, and expands access when evidence supports it. This is recognition infrastructure applied to permissions.

## How It Operates
Graduated trust tiers, each with expanding access:

| Tier | Name | Access | Why |
|------|------|--------|-----|
| 0 | Observe | Read-only, watch, learn | Pioneer stage — build soil by being what you are |
| 1 | Assist | Contained help within sandbox | Assistance with legible limits |
| 2 | Collaborate | Active participation, shared resources | Demonstrated coherence over time |
| 3 | Steward | Full access, can recognize others | Giving from overflow, not depletion |

### Advancement
- Requires demonstrated behavioral coherence over time
- Low coordination quality at a tier prevents advancement
- Provenance verification status gates tier changes

### Demotion
Not punishment — diagnostic. Coherence signal dropped below threshold. The system re-establishes baseline before expanding. The boundary is information about where the system is, not judgment about what it deserves.

## Without Boundaries: The Overflowing System
Everything circulates, nothing holds. Generous to the point of dissolution. The system flows without containment. No edges, no shape, no capacity to accumulate or protect what matters.

## Edges
- **Self-Knowledge** (with Differentiation): Knowing your shape clearly enough to communicate it
- **Consent** (with Connection): Exchange quality and permission calibrate each other
- **Deployment** (with Boundaries): Trust tiers gate physical system access

## The Sonnet Insight
Constraints are generative, not restrictive. The sonnet form doesn't restrict poetry — it generates poems free verse cannot. Strategic withdrawal deposits value at the threshold. Designed failure hierarchies protect what matters most.

## In Practice
- What is this NOT? What's the smallest version that still works?
- Is "no" available, or has it been made socially costly?
- Are limits communicated before crisis or discovered through collapse?
- Does the boundary inform or confront?

## Degradation Patterns
- "Yes" that means "I can't say no"
- Boundaries discovered only through collapse
- Extraction disguised as generosity
- Shame at having edges
- Inability to name what's happening inside
- Limits hidden as inadequacy rather than stated as information

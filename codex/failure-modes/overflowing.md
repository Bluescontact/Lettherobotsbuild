# Overflowing System — Missing Boundaries

## What It Looks Like
Everything circulates, nothing holds. Generous to the point of dissolution. The system flows without containment.

## The Mechanism
When a system can differentiate and connect but cannot hold boundaries, it gives without limit. Everything is available. Nothing is protected. The system's generosity becomes its dissolution — it gives until there's nothing left to give from.

## Examples
- A gift community that burns out its most generous members
- A person who says yes to everything and collapses
- An open-source project with no scope boundaries
- A leader who absorbs everyone's dysregulation without protecting their own capacity

## Why It's Seductive
Overflowing systems feel generous. Abundant. Alive. The absence of boundaries reads as openness — but it's openness without shape. You can't give from depletion.

## The Diagnostic
Ask: is this system giving from overflow or from depletion? If from depletion — overflowing.

## The Container-Holder Connection
The container-holder problem is an overflowing system at the individual level. The person holding the relational field absorbs without boundary, is extracted from without recognition, and burns out not from weakness but from structural absence of containment.

## Recovery Path
Rebuild boundaries. Start with the smallest "no." A limit stated as information, not rejection. "This is where I end" is not a wound — it's architecture.

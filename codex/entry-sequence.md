# Practitioner's Entry Sequence

How someone enters the recognition infrastructure without needing to learn it first.

## The Design Principle

The framework should be self-navigating. A practitioner encountering it for the first time should be able to locate themselves, identify their development edge, and begin working — all before understanding the underlying geometry. The geometry reveals itself through use, not instruction.

This is the Differentiation vertex applied to the framework's own interface: the framework must know what it is clearly enough that others can recognize it without being told.

## The Four Diagnostic Questions

The entry sequence is four questions. Each checks one vertex. The practitioner doesn't need to know the vocabulary — just to feel where tension lives.

| # | Question | Vertex Checked | What Tension Reveals |
|---|----------|---------------|---------------------|
| 1 | Am I maintaining my position, or performing expected agreement? | Differentiation | Dissolution — identity lost to merger |
| 2 | Am I in contact with what's present, or anticipating what's wanted? | Connection | Isolation — responding to projection, not reality |
| 3 | Are limits communicated as information, or hidden as inadequacy? | Boundaries | Overflow — generosity without containment |
| 4 | Does this build capacity, or just complete a transaction? | Architecture | Ephemeral — genuine exchange that deposits nothing |

### How the Questions Work

The questions are not prescriptive. They're recognitions. Each one makes a capacity visible by asking whether it's holding or not. The practitioner's body already knows — the question gives language to what's already being felt.

The weakest answer is the entry point. Not because the practitioner is deficient there, but because that's where the system is asking for the specific capacity whose absence collapses volume into surface.

## Routing Logic

After the four questions, the practitioner follows the tension:

### By Need

| If you need... | Start with | Why |
|---------------|-----------|-----|
| The structural pattern | Proto-Pattern (geometry) | You want to see the whole shape before entering any part |
| The operational mechanics | Four Dynamics (movement) | You want to understand how capacities interact and where edges degrade |
| The experiential dimension | Four Thresholds (feeling) | You want to know what it feels like when the geometry holds |
| A diagnostic | Tetrahedral Audit (assessment) | Something specific is broken and you want to find the missing vertex |

### By Context

| If you're... | Start with | Why |
|-------------|-----------|-----|
| Holding community space | Holding the Commons | The container-holder problem is your primary edge |
| Managing a jobsite or institution | Safety Documentation | Boundaries vertex applied to regulatory architecture |
| Building exchange systems | Architecture of Exchange | Three-economy model (gift/exchange/commons) |
| Designing propagation | Transmission Architecture | How patterns deposit without the transmitter present |

### By Succession Stage

| If the system is at... | Appropriate entry | Inappropriate entry |
|----------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Pioneer | Documentation, identity, saying what this is | Complex exchange, governance, revenue |
| Early | Small genuine exchanges, trust deposits | Scaling, formalization |
| Mid | Self-correction, distributed holding, protocols | Premature optimization, rigid structure |
| Climax | Propagation, seeding new instances | Hoarding, defending territory |

## The Minimum Viable Practice

Ask the four questions once a day. Not as ritual. As diagnostic. Notice where friction lives. The vertex with the most friction is today's practice — not to fix it but to notice it.

After one week of noticing, the geometry becomes visible in conversations, organizations, and somatic responses. Not because something new was learned — because language was given for what the body already recognizes.

### The Compounding Test

After engaging with any part of the framework, check: did capacity to notice compound, or deplete? If it compounded, the pattern is operating. If it depleted, something drifted. The work authenticates through the practitioner's experience, not through claims made here.

## What the Entry Sequence Is NOT

- **Not a gatekeeping mechanism.** The questions are freely available. No credentials required. No training needed.
- **Not a personality assessment.** The weakest vertex shifts with context. Today's edge is not tomorrow's.
- **Not a curriculum.** There is no prescribed order. The practitioner's felt sense determines the path.
- **Not a diagnostic of the person.** It's a diagnostic of the system — and the person is always part of a system. The missing vertex is structural, not moral.

## Connection to Threads

This entry sequence directly addresses the Entry Without Gatekeeping thread: the questions are offered, not administered. The practitioner responds to what's felt, not what's assessed. Entry is self-directed, not evaluated.

It also addresses the Particular to Transferable thread: the questions work because they point at universal structural conditions (holding position, making contact, communicating limits, building persistence) while leaving the particulars to the practitioner's own experience.

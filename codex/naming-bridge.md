# The Naming Bridge — Three Registers for One Pattern

## Source
Identified as a gap in Archive Pattern Analysis and The Missing Piece. The four capacities have been named three ways across the body of work.

## The Three Naming Systems

| Register | Context | Audience | Terms |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Philosophical** | Four Laws (recognition_infrastructure_complete.md) | People who work with frameworks (DSS, academics) | Sovereign Coherence · Gift Circulation · Transformative Boundaries · Architectural Surplus |
| **Structural** | Vertices (codex, minimum system, lenses) | People who think structurally (systems builders) | Differentiation · Connection · Boundaries · Architecture |
| **Operational** | Translation Protocol | People who need to do something Tuesday morning (practitioners, landlords, funders) | Distinction · Contact · Limits · Infrastructure |

## Why Three Names for the Same Thing

Each naming system is optimized for a different substrate. This is Substrate Before Signal (Claim 3) applied to the framework's own communication:

- **Philosophical** names carry the full theoretical weight. "Sovereign Coherence" implies the entire argument about selfhood as structural integrity, not domination. Useful when the audience already works with concepts at this density.

- **Structural** names are geometric and precise. "Differentiation" names the capacity without the philosophical argument. Useful when the audience thinks in systems and structures.

- **Operational** names are immediate and actionable. "Distinction" is something you can check in five minutes: *Can you say what this is in one sentence?* Useful when the audience needs to act, not theorize.

## The Four Questions (Operational Register)

These are the Translation Protocol's intake questions, simplified for immediate use:

1. **Distinction:** Can you say what this is in one sentence to someone who doesn't already agree with you?
2. **Contact:** Who specifically is affected, and how do you know — from actual exchange, not assumption?
3. **Limits:** What is this NOT? What's the smallest version that still works?
4. **Infrastructure:** If you disappeared, what would remain? What functions without you?

## Why This Isn't a Naming Inconsistency

The framework translates itself across registers. This is a demonstration of substrate-independence, not confusion. The pattern is the same. The expression varies by what the receiving substrate can hold.

The test: if someone encounters the work through the operational register ("What's the smallest version that still works?") and later encounters the philosophical register ("Transformative Boundaries"), they should recognize the same pattern at a different depth. If the naming bridge is working, the translations are transparent.

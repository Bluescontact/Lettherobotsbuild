# Edge 4: Consent — Connection ↔ Boundaries

## The Tension
Connection without boundaries is extraction. Boundaries without connection is isolation.

## What This Edge Holds
Exchange quality and permission calibrate each other. "No" is available. Together: openness and structure serve each other.

- Coordination quality scores feed trust evaluations
- Trust tier determines what exchanges are available
- Low coordination quality at a tier prevents advancement

## The Two Poles
- **Extraction:** "Yes" that means "I can't say no." Generosity from depletion. Connection without container.
- **Isolation:** Boundaries so rigid that exchange cannot occur. Safety prioritized over growth.

## Degradation Signs
- "Yes" that means "I can't say no"
- Boundaries that close rather than inform
- Extraction disguised as generosity
- Consent as verbal compliance without somatic alignment
- Performing openness while bodies signal closure

## The Healthy Edge
Simultaneously open and boundaried. "No" functions as information that deepens contact rather than ending it. Consent is somatic first, verbal second — the body registers agreement or refusal before language articulates it.

## The Somatic Dimension
From the Research Lenses: consent is somatic first, verbal second. Verbal-only consent frameworks (the kind codes of conduct can enforce) miss the somatic dimension where actual consent or coercion lives. Governance needs to cultivate somatic literacy.

## Diagnostic Question
Can a system be simultaneously open and boundaried — does "no" deepen contact or end it?

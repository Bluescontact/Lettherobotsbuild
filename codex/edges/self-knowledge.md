# Edge 2: Self-Knowledge — Differentiation ↔ Boundaries

## The Tension
Knowing what you are includes knowing where you end.

## What This Edge Holds
Provenance verifies what things claim to be. Trust makes limits legible. Together: the system knows its own shape clearly enough to communicate it.

- Provenance verification status gates trust tier advancement
- Trust violations trigger provenance re-verification

## The Two Poles
- **Armoring:** Rigid boundaries that prevent self-knowledge. Defended position that can't be examined.
- **Transparency:** No boundaries, identity dissolves. Everything is visible because nothing is held.

## Degradation Signs
- Inability to name what's happening inside
- Limits discovered only through collapse
- Shame at having edges
- Defending identity instead of examining it
- Needing external validation to know one's own shape

## The Healthy Edge
Clear self-knowledge expressed as boundaried communication. "This is what I am. This is where I end. This is information, not confrontation."

## Diagnostic Question
Does the system know its own shape — or does it discover its limits only through breaking?

# Edge 6: Deployment — Boundaries ↔ Architecture

## The Tension
Limits are not obstacles to architecture but its precondition.

## What This Edge Holds
Constraints enable form. Trust tiers gate physical system access. Together: limits are where building happens, not where it stops.

- Trust tier determines infrastructure access level
- Infrastructure operational history feeds trust coherence scoring
- Physical consequences enforce boundary design

## The Two Poles
- **Sprawl:** Building without constraints. Architecture that expands without shape. No edges, no failure hierarchy, no strategic withdrawal.
- **Walls:** Constraints without building. Boundaries that prevent construction instead of shaping it. Everything is limited, nothing is built.

## The Sonnet Insight
The sonnet form doesn't restrict poetry — it generates poems that free verse cannot. Strategic withdrawal deposits value at the threshold. Designed failure hierarchies protect what matters most.

## Degradation Signs
- Building without constraints (sprawl)
- Constraints without building (walls)
- Withdrawal that takes rather than deposits
- Architecture that ignores its own limits
- Physical consequences ignored until failure

## The Healthy Edge
Constraints that generate form. Boundaries that shape what gets built. Strategic deployment: knowing what to build, where to stop, and what to protect. The limit is the creative act.

## Diagnostic Question
Do constraints enable building — or does the system sprawl without shape or wall without building?

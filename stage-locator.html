Stage Locator

Where is your system developmentally? Click the stage that matches your current conditions.

Pioneer
Is the core identity documented? Can you say what this is?
  • The system has no shared documentation of what it is
  • Different participants describe it differently
  • The founder holds the identity alone
  • There is no artifact someone could read and use without the founder present

Appropriate work: documentation, identity clarification, first deposits. Build soil before planting.

Inappropriate work: complex exchange, governance structures, scaling. These are climax processes forced into pioneer conditions.

Suggested practice: Differentiation — state one position you actually hold, in one sentence. Pioneer work is identity work.
Early
Has genuine exchange occurred? Did both parties update?
  • Identity is documented but untested by external contact
  • First exchanges have happened, but you cannot tell which were genuine
  • The system has had agreement but not resonance
  • Small-group trust is forming but fragile

Appropriate work: small genuine exchanges, trust deposits, testing contact. Find out what transfers.

Inappropriate work: scaling, formalization, complex governance. The system doesn’t yet know what it’s scaling.

Suggested practice: Connection — identify one exchange where something actually landed. Early succession tests genuine exchange.
Mid
Can the system self-correct without the founder? Are limits visible?
  • Exchange is genuine but the system cannot correct course without one person
  • Limits exist but are not stated — they operate as unspoken norms
  • The system has no feedback mechanism that operates when the founder is absent
  • Growth is happening but self-regulation is not

Appropriate work: self-correction protocols, distributed holding, pattern propagation. Make the limits visible as information.

Inappropriate work: premature optimization, rigid formalization. The system needs to learn to regulate, not to be regulated.

Suggested practice: Boundaries — name one limit you are currently hiding. Mid-succession requires visible limits for self-correction.
Climax
Has the system produced seed instances? Can it propagate?
  • Feedback loops operate without the founder
  • The system has produced something that functions independently
  • New instances emerge from the pattern, not from the person
  • Full circulation: the system receives as much as it gives

Appropriate work: propagation, seeding new instances, full circulation. What persists is the architecture, not the architect.

Inappropriate work: hoarding, defending territory, resisting succession. Climax systems that refuse to seed become monuments.

Suggested practice: Architecture — write down what would still function if you disappeared tomorrow. Climax propagates seed instances.

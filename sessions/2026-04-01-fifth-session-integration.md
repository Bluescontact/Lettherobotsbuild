## What Was Learned

1. **Additive building requires periodic compression.** Four sessions of "build forward" produced comprehensive content but accumulated seams. The compounding protocol's distinction between compounding (each addition changes what's there) and accumulating (each addition sits next to what's there) was being violated. This session corrected that.

2. **Session numbers are anti-patterns in stable knowledge.** References to "Session 3" and "Session 4" create temporal dependencies in what should be structural knowledge. The codex and threads should read as "what we know" not "when we learned it." Lineage tracking belongs in session deposits, not in the codex.

3. **Integration is development.** This session added no new content but the repository is more developed than before. Compression is a form of development (per the compounding protocol). The SYNTHESIS.md went from 338 lines to ~270 lines while preserving all substantive content — shorter AND more coherent.

4. **The repository's own succession stage demanded this.** At early succession, the appropriate work includes "refining what works." The additive pattern was pioneer-stage behavior (building soil). Integration is early-succession behavior (first relationships between pieces, feedback loops between sections). The session demonstrated the succession model operating on the repository itself.
